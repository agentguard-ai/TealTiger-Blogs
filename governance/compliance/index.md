Compliance Enablement defines how TealTiger makes AI governance **auditable, provable, and operational**, without relying on retrospective reporting or manual audit preparation.

For agentic AI systems, compliance cannot be achieved through documentation alone. Controls must be enforced at execution time, and evidence must be generated by default. This hub focuses on **compliance as a system capability**, not a reporting exercise.

## What This Hub Covers

- The difference between compliance enablement and compliance reporting
- Preparing for emerging AI regulations without over‑claiming compliance
- Audit readiness for autonomous and agentic AI systems
- Runtime enforcement, approvals, and fail‑closed behavior
- Evidence‑first compliance posture

## Why Compliance Enablement Matters

Without compliance enablement:

- Audits rely on explanations instead of proof
- Controls are inferred rather than demonstrated
- Regulatory readiness becomes reactive
- Evidence is assembled manually and inconsistently
- Trust erodes as systems become more autonomous

TealTiger treats compliance as a **by‑product of deterministic governance and evidence**, not a separate process.
