Frameworks define the **external expectations** for AI governance — what regulators, auditors, and standards bodies require systems to demonstrate.

For agentic AI, frameworks cannot be satisfied through documentation alone. They must be translated into **deterministic enforcement points** and **audit‑grade evidence** that operate at runtime. This hub explains how governance frameworks become operational system capabilities.

## What This Hub Covers

- Translating AI governance frameworks into enforceable controls
- Operationalizing NIST AI RMF for agentic systems
- Why frameworks fail without runtime enforcement
- Mapping framework requirements to evidence and enforcement
- Avoiding checkbox compliance and over‑mapping

## Why Frameworks Matter

Without proper operationalization:

- Framework alignment remains theoretical
- Controls exist only on paper
- Audits rely on interpretation instead of proof
- Enforcement gaps remain hidden
- Compliance confidence erodes

TealTiger treats frameworks as **inputs to system design**, not reporting artifacts.
