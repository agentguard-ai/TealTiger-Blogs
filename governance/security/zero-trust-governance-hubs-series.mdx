# Zero Trust Across TealTiger Governance Hubs

This series treats **Zero Trust as a governance pattern**, not a security feature.
Each article anchors Zero Trust to a specific **TealTiger Governance Hub**, using an institutional, cloud‑native lens with **deterministic enforcement** at the core.

> **TealTiger perspective:**
> Zero Trust governs execution. Security, cost control, auditability, compliance enablement, and sustainability are consequences.

This page serves as the **series overview**. Each section below will expand into a dedicated article under its respective governance hub.

---

## 1) Runtime Governance (Agents & Execution Boundaries)

**Proposed article:** *Zero Trust as Runtime Governance for Agentic Systems*

**Abstract**
Agentic systems amplify risk when autonomy is unbounded. Zero Trust reframes autonomy as conditional execution—every step is explicitly authorized, context‑aware, and revocable.

**Key ideas**
- Identity‑first agents: if it executes, it has an identity
- Explicit tool allowlists and scope‑bound permissions
- Purpose‑ and time‑bound execution
- Assume‑breach applied to agents

**What Zero Trust governs**
- Which tools an agent may invoke
- What data it may access
- When execution must pause, escalate, or halt

**Outcome**
Deterministic runtime enforcement replaces post‑hoc review. Autonomy exists—within verified boundaries.

## 2) Cost & Economic Governance

**Proposed article:** *Zero Trust for Economic Blast‑Radius Control in AI Systems*

**Abstract**
Unchecked trust becomes unchecked spend. Zero Trust brings authorization discipline to cost by treating every unit of compute as a governed decision.

**Key ideas**
- Explicit approval for high‑cost actions
- Budget‑aware execution policies
- Contextual throttling and kill‑switches
- Preventing runaway agents and infinite loops

**What Zero Trust governs**
- Who can spend
- How much can be spent
- Under what conditions spending stops

**Outcome**
FinOps becomes a runtime governance concern—not a monthly reconciliation.

## 3) Audit & Evidence (Beyond Logging)

**Proposed article:** *From Logs to Proof: Zero Trust as an Evidence Engine*

**Abstract**
Logs describe what happened. Governance requires proof of why it happened. Zero Trust produces decision evidence, not just telemetry.

**Key ideas**
- Deterministic policy evaluation trails
- Context captured at decision time
- Replayable authorization logic
- Auditability by design

**What Zero Trust governs**
- Access decisions
- Policy versions in effect
- Contextual inputs and outcomes

**Outcome**
Audits shift from forensic reconstruction to verifiable assurance.

---

## 4) Security as a Governance Domain

**Proposed article:** *Security Failures Are Governance Failures: A Zero Trust View*

**Abstract**
Security is not a separate layer—it is an outcome of good governance. Zero Trust embeds prevention directly into policy design and enforcement.

**Key ideas**
- No implicit internal trust
- Least privilege as a design default
- Micro‑segmented execution paths
- Preventive, not reactive, controls

**What Zero Trust governs**
- Lateral movement
- Privilege accumulation
- Credential sprawl

**Outcome**
Security incidents are reduced by eliminating trust assumptions at the governance layer.

---

## 5) Frameworks & Standards (Reference)

**Proposed article:** *Zero Trust as a Design Principle Across Governance Frameworks*

**Abstract**
Zero Trust is not a compliance framework, but it operationalizes many. This article maps Zero Trust patterns to widely used standards without overclaiming.

**Key ideas**
- NIST SP 800‑207
- NIST AI RMF
- ISO/IEC 27001 and 42001
- EU AI Act (compliance‑enabling, not certifying)

**What Zero Trust governs**
- Access control intent
- Evidence generation
- Risk containment

**Outcome**
Frameworks remain references; Zero Trust becomes the execution discipline.

---

## 6) Sustainability & Environmental Impact

**Proposed article:** *Zero Trust for Sustainable AI Operations*

**Abstract**
Implicit trust wastes compute. Zero Trust enforces discipline that reduces unnecessary execution, cost, and environmental impact.

**Key ideas**
- Purpose‑bound execution
- Early termination on context violation
- Eliminating redundant retries

**What Zero Trust governs**
- When work should not run
- How long it may run
- Why it runs at all

**Outcome**
Sustainability emerges as a governance outcome—not an afterthought.
